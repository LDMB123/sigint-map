CORE DESIGN SYSTEM AT A GLANCE
================================================================================

COLORS (Hex Codes)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Backgrounds:        #faf8f3 (cream) | #f5f1e8 (beige)
Text:               #000000 (black) | #2d2d2d (dark gray)
CTA Button:         #d97706 (orange) ← THE action color, use sparingly
CTA Hover:          #c9600f (darker orange)
Secondary:          #27633f (green) | #1e3a5f (blue)
Muted Text:         #8b8680 (warm gray) | #666666 (medium gray)

Philosophy: Earth tones evoke vinyl records and concert programs. Orange is
the ONLY bold accent color. Everything else is sophisticated and muted.

FONTS (The Trinity)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Display:            DS Moster (bold, distinctive, memorable, rock & roll)
Heading:            Bluu Next Bold (modern, confident, authoritative)
Accent:             Special Elite (typewriter feel, nostalgic, personal)
Body:               Clean sans-serif (readable, modern, accessible)

Philosophy: Three-font combination is uniquely recognizable as DMB. Creates
visual richness while maintaining authenticity and accessibility.

SPACING (8px Base Unit)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4px (xs)     | 8px (sm)     | 12px (md)    | 16px (lg)
20px (xl)    | 24px (2xl)   | 32px (3xl)   | 48px (5xl)

RESPONSIVE BREAKPOINTS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Mobile:     320–767px   (1 column, 16px padding)
Tablet:     768–1023px  (2 column, 20px gutter)
Desktop:    1024–1439px (3 column, 24px gutter)
Large:      1440px+     (4 column, 28px gutter, max-width constrained)

BUTTON STYLES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Primary CTA:        #d97706 orange background, white text, 12px 24px padding
Secondary:          #1e3a5f blue background, white text, same sizing
Tertiary:           #27633f green background, white text, same sizing
Text Button:        Transparent background, orange text, underline on hover

KEY BRAND PRINCIPLES
================================================================================

WHAT MAKES DMB DESIGN WORK
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
✓ Warm, organic, analog-inspired colors and textures
✓ Candid concert/crowd photography with golden color grading
✓ All three fonts used in combination (distinctive)
✓ Tape graphics framing major sections (signature element)
✓ Community and shared experience emphasized throughout
✓ Fans treated like family, not customers
✓ 30+ years of history respected equally
✓ Design that won't look dated in 5 years (vintage aesthetic is timeless)

WHAT BREAKS THE BRAND
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
✗ Corporate marketing language ("leverage," "synergy," "content")
✗ Trendy design patterns that feel dated in 12 months
✗ Over-polished design that removes authenticity
✗ Forgetting live concert experience is central to brand
✗ Artificial scarcity or FOMO tactics
✗ Overly-posed or staged photography
✗ Treating fans as customers, not community
✗ Multiple competing accent colors (orange is the only bold action color)

CORE BRAND VALUES (Check Every Design Against These)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. AUTHENTICITY
   Real, unpolished, human. No corporate manufacturing.

2. COMMUNITY
   The Warehouse fan community. Shared experiences. Belonging.

3. LIVE EXPERIENCE
   The magic of live performance, improvisation, the moment.

4. GIVING BACK
   Bama Works Foundation. $65M+ raised. Purpose beyond profit.

5. SUSTAINABILITY
   Environmental responsibility. Greening the touring industry.

6. MUSICAL EXCELLENCE
   Virtuosity. Jazz-influenced. Genre-defying artistry.

7. LONGEVITY
   30+ years of history. Evolution while staying true.

ACCESSIBILITY REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

COLOR CONTRAST
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Black on cream:     21:1 ✓✓✓ Excellent
Dark gray on cream: 10:1 ✓✓ Excellent
Orange on white:    8:1 ✓ Good (but test on actual backgrounds)
Light gray:         Avoid for body text (too low contrast)
Requirement:        WCAG AA minimum 4.5:1 for all body text

TYPOGRAPHY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Body Text Minimum:  16px (14px acceptable for secondary only)
Line Height:        1.5–1.6 for body readability
Font Weight:        Avoid < 400 for body text
Fallback Fonts:     Always specified for custom fonts

INTERACTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Focus State:        Visible on all buttons/links (not just hover)
Keyboard Nav:       Tab through entire page, all items accessible
Touch Targets:      44px minimum height/width on mobile
Form Labels:        Associated with inputs (for/id attributes)
Error Messages:     Clearly associated with form fields
Heading Hierarchy:  Logical (h1 → h2 → h3, no skipped levels)

TESTING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
□ Chrome DevTools Lighthouse (Accessibility tab)
□ WAVE WebAIM contrast checker
□ Axe DevTools automated testing
□ Manual keyboard navigation (Tab through)
□ Screen reader testing (NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver)
□ Real device testing (not just browser emulation)

VERIFICATION CHECKLIST (Quick Version)
================================================================================

BEFORE DESIGN REVIEW
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
□ Colors match hex codes exactly (Quick Reference or Analysis)
□ All three fonts used (not just one or two)
□ Tape graphics present in major section breaks
□ Orange used ONLY for CTAs (not decoration)
□ Warm color grading visible in images
□ Photography is candid, not overly-posed
□ Community values reflected in layout/content
□ Navigation structure clear and accessible
□ Responsive breakpoints tested (mobile, tablet, desktop)
□ Accessibility: text contrast verified (4.5:1 minimum)

BEFORE DEVELOPER HANDOFF
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
□ All component states defined (hover, focus, active, disabled)
□ Spacing consistent with 8px base unit
□ Typography hierarchy logical and implemented
□ Colors defined using design tokens
□ All assets provided (fonts, tape graphics, icons, images)
□ Responsive behavior documented for all breakpoints
□ Accessibility requirements confirmed
□ Design passes Brand Alignment Checklist

BEFORE STAKEHOLDER APPROVAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
□ All team sign-offs completed (designer, developer, QA)
□ Brand Alignment Checklist fully verified
□ Final "smell test" question answered: "Does a longtime DMB fan
  immediately recognize this as part of the DMB universe?"
□ Any brand deviations documented with justification
□ Accessibility and performance targets met
□ Ready for launch

DESIGN SYSTEM SCORING RUBRIC
================================================================================

9–10: DMB APPROVED
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Hits all brand pillars (authenticity, community, excellence, etc.)
• Distinctive and immediately recognizable as DMB
• Every element serves a purpose
• Would be proud to show Dave himself
• Strongly reinforces brand identity

7–8: ON BRAND
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Hits most major brand pillars
• Clearly uses DMB visual language
• Minor tweaks might strengthen
• Comfortable presenting to stakeholders
• Good brand reinforcement

5–6: ACCEPTABLE BUT NEEDS WORK
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Hits some brand pillars
• Generic in some areas
• Could be any band's site
• Requires revision before launch
• Needs stronger brand connection

3–4: OFF BRAND
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Missing key visual elements
• Generic or corporate feel
• Significant redesign needed
• Not acceptable for launch
• Needs complete rethinking

1–2: COMPLETELY OFF BRAND
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Looks nothing like DMB
• Contradicts brand values
• Start over
• Unacceptable for launch
• Needs fundamental rethinking

THE ULTIMATE QUESTION
================================================================================

Before approving ANY design, ask:

"Would a longtime DMB fan look at this and immediately recognize it as part
of the DMB universe, or would they think it was built by a generic music
streaming service?"

If the answer is the latter, send it back for revision.
