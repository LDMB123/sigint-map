# Speakeasy Prompt Pack v7 - Ultra Think Physics Design Notes

## 1. Goal
Deliver 20 rewritten prompts (21-40) that maximize:
- photorealism through first-principles physics constraints,
- provocative but non-explicit editorial style,
- composition consistency with 70-80% subject occupancy.

## 2. First-Principles Analysis
### 2.1 Actual Problem
Previous outputs looked stylized because prompts over-indexed on adjectives and under-specified physical constraints and contact mechanics.

### 2.2 Assumptions Challenged
| Assumption | Challenge | Result |
|---|---|---|
| "More realism comes from saying photorealistic repeatedly." | Realism is produced by coherent geometry, optics, material response, and pose biomechanics. | Added mandatory physics stack.
| "More controversy only means less coverage." | Visual controversy can come from styling language, power framing, and anti-establishment context. | Kept daring styling while staying non-explicit.
| "One prompt body can fit all scenes." | Each environment has different force, contact, and light behavior. | Added per-prompt kinematic chains and scene anchors.

### 2.3 Fundamental Truths Used
- Human pose plausibility depends on center-of-mass projection and joint limits.
- Cloth appearance depends on anisotropic stretch, seam tension, inertia, and damping.
- Believable images require light transport consistency: shadows, reflections, and exposure must match geometry.
- Framing controls perceived authority and realism; occupancy constraints reduce drift.

## 3. v7 Prompt Architecture
Each prompt now includes:
1. Identity lock.
2. Controversy vector (adult, non-explicit).
3. Sensual pose blueprint (non-explicit).
4. First-principles physics stack.
5. Scene anchors.
6. Hard two-piece attire requirement.
7. Prompt-specific kinematic chain.
8. Camera profile with realistic lens behavior.
9. Hard reject criteria.
10. Safe backup preserving physics and style with moderated risk.

## 4. Adaptive Strategy Execution
### Baseline Observations
- Prior packs improved style but still drifted in anatomy, contact realism, and consistency.
- Some runs failed due instability under highly aggressive prompt language.

### Adaptations Applied
1. Switched from style-heavy language to solver-style constraints (biomechanics, cloth, optics).
2. Added global anti-synthetic lock (no plastic skin, no over-smoothing, no fake reflections).
3. Added prompt-specific kinematic chain for each scene to force physically grounded variation.
4. Kept safe fallback prompts to preserve throughput when primary fails.
5. Standardized composition lock to 70-80% occupancy in all prompts.
